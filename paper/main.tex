\begin{abstract}
The spread of misinformation on social networks threatens societal stability by shaping public opinion and collective action. This paper develops a game-theoretic model in which agents, motivated by reputational rewards, interact with information and update their actions over time. These actions are applied to a structured social network where diffusion is governed by agentsâ€™ influence and reach. By capturing how individual decisions aggregate across diverse topologies, the model quantifies contamination rates within neighborhoods after repeated updates, highlighting how utility-driven behaviors shape the velocity and extent of misinformation spread.

\end{abstract}
